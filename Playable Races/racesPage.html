Humans

Humans. The number one most prolific humanoid race on the planet. Considering the nature of their squishiness and the dangers the world of Loreheim holds one would normally assume these rabid little chihuahuas would have gone extinct by now, but no. They're somehow just as stubbornly adaptive as they are prolific.


BENEFITS:
+1 to three stats of your choosing
Adaptive: You can start in any location of your choosing and take no debuffs from your home environment (eg. If you start in the dessert you'll get advantage on saves to withstand extreme heat, but disadvantage to withstand the cold)

DRAWBACKS:
Squishy and tasty with ketchup: Monsters will see you as easy prey and attack you more frequently and first if they can easily get to you.

Dwarves

Deep within the world dwells the race of dwarves. They are famous for being as hardy and stubborn as the stone they build their cities from, along with their incredible craftsmanship. Speaking of craftsmanship, their talent for it has nothing to do with skill or any kind of training, though they do hone their craft to perfection. Rather, their ability to shape the stones and metals of Loreheim comes from their reproductive instincts. When a female dwarf is ready to have a child they travel to the "Core" with family and sometimes very close friends. The core is a living mass of stone that carries high quantities of mana. Each community has a core, and every dwarf who lives around the core was carved from it. When a new dwarf is being carved the relatives will stand guard and watch until the mother is done shaping her new child, and once they are fully formed the watchers will each have the chance to carve a rune upon the child's body representing a positive trait (eg. Strong, bold, loyal, etc.). Once done the core will "breathe life" into the child and the mother will return to the community and raise the child to adulthood. The entire process is considered a sacred tradition that not even all relatives are guaranteed a chance to witness. No one really knows how or why the process works, and no outsider has ever been allowed close enough to study it. As the children grow the marks given to them take on the appearance of tattoos while they make the transition of stone to flesh over the course of a week or two.



BENEFITS:
+2 to con and +1 to str
Child of stone: +2 to Con saves vs poisons and drugs

DRAWBACKS:
Alien: Many of the more "natural" races think of you as alien and unnatural due to your origins and may not be very welcoming towards you.

Fae

The Fae come in many shapes and sizes depending on the race they mutated from after the Astre fell to their continent and brought their unique mana with them. This variety ranges from the savage western Orcs all the way to the now extinct Elves and some who's race of origin is indiscernible, such as the bone and tooth fae. Below are a few of the possible variants you could play as, but be warned, you won't be welcomed with open arms by many of the civilized races (especially humans) due to your otherworldly, and in some cases, disturbing nature. Among their many unique traits a common one all fae share is that they stop aging upon reaching maturity. Their growth rates differ based upon their race of origin, but it always stops once they're mature. Just as with all other mortals they can still perish to illness, injury, and other natural causes excluding age.




Elven Fae: Having originated from the now extinct race of elves, elven fae retained the ethereal beauty elves used to be famous for and then some. Much like the rest of their kind, these fae have a penchant for meat, but to a much higher and more magical degree than the others like them. Through the years these fae have developed the ability to consume the memories of those they cannibalize. While most other sentient races view this as a horrifying act, the fae simply see it as a special tradition and a great responsibility to carry on the memories of those before them.

BENEFITS:
+2 cha, +1 con
Consume: When consuming the flesh of a newly deceased humanoid you can gain flashes of various memories from the beings past. WARNING, just because you can eat raw flesh doesn't mean you're immune to diseases that can be transmitted by corpses and certain body parts.

DRAWBACKS:
Cannibal: You're a monster in the eyes of the people despite your unparalleled beauty. If you're recognized as an elven fae you will, at best, be avoided like the plague, and at worst driven out of the community with torches, pitchforks, and death threats.


Irkyne: The Irkyne are a group of warrior fae that roam the Fae Isle in clans. These fearsome creatures were born from orcs and similarly large and intimidating races. They are most well known for their hulking size, often standing upwards of seven feet tall with varying shades of skin ranging from blue to green to even obsidian black in some cases. That paired with their horns that differ from group to group makes them quite the terrifying opponent.

BENEFITS:
+3 str, +1 con, -1 cha
Brute: What you lack in charm you more than make up for in shear size and strength. Gain a +2 to strength based checks and saves.

DRAWBACKS:
Beast: Your strange and fearsome appearance makes even the bravest of souls wary of you.


Nixie: These small creatures stand typically around three to four feet tall and bear the appearance of wild children who were raised by nature herself. While they look humanoid, their origins are a mystery and they also give off the unique feeling most fae do, often making the few who have the luck (or misfortune) of meeting one instantly on edge. Upon closer inspection they have been noted to have large eyes that reflect light and green tinted skin. The vines, leaves, and sometimes moss that many assume to have caught in their hair actually grows from it naturally.

BENEFITS:
+1 dex, +2 wis
Nature's favorite: As a Nixie you are gifted with the ability to hear the voices of animals and plants. While you can't speak the same language as the animals and plants (rendering communication impossible) you can get a general idea of what they're trying to communicate most of the time. Survival checks are made with a +2 and animals are more friendly towards you as long as you aren't actively appearing as a threat to them.

DRAWBACKS:
Uncivilized: Speaking with civilized races isn't your strong suite, so checks that require you to interact with people are made with a -2.

Lizardfolk

There are two variations of Lizardfolk. The Valkin and the Ferin. The Valkin live in extremely humid areas, like the jungles of NosVite. They come in almost every color known to man and grow a similarly vibrant array of feathers on the back of their head and carry lethal poisons that they use to strike down prey and enemies alike. Valkin tend to live in small tribes in homes built high above the ground using woven together branches to form their nests. They're also extremely territorial and aggressive. The Ferin are more partial to living on the coasts near settlements. They often do trade with small part towns and cities who benefit from the added security of living in coexistence with the Ferin. While much more easy going than their Valkin cousins, the Ferin are just as capable of violence, if not more so. Also unlike the Valkin, Ferin come in dull blue and grey hues and are built more for swimming than climbing.


Valkin
BENEFITS:
+2 str, +1 con
Poisonous: 1/day you can make a normal melee attack to bite a creature. If you succeed, the enemy must make a con save or suffer 1d6 + con poison damage until they make their save, get cured, or die.

DRAWBACKS:
Violent: As the name of the trait states, you're kind are known for incredible violence, especially when it comes to territorial disputes. This has led to some unfortunate situations and a general distrust among the other races towards your kind.


Ferin
BENEFITS:
+2 dex, +1 str
Adept swimmer: Having lived in the water all your life you've gotten used to swimming at top speeds to keep away from predators or to catch fish. Your swim speed is the same as your walking speed and you can hold your breath for up to three hours.

DRAWBACKS:
Aquatic: Ferin tend to spend more time under water than not, so it's usually quite uncomfortable to be on land for them and takes a lot of time to adjust to the change in environment.

Fallen Astre

The Astre are heavenly beings with large white wings and red eyes, otherwise appearing similar to humans. It is said that they were created when Itris plucked the brightest and most beautiful stars from the sky and shaped them into souls before placing them within the vessels she had created for her perfect soldiers. The Astre were created with the sole purpose of being her hands, eyes, shields, and swords in the mortal realm. They offered guidance to their goddesses chosen few and struck down those who offended her. This is how it always was, and how it seemed it would always be, until one day a handful of Astre started to become frustrated with the way they were being treated, as if they were mere tools and nothing more. They confronted their goddess over the matter, and as a result she became enraged and threw them down from the Celestial realm, Regna Caelum. These fallen Astre now wander Loreheim, some seeking redemption, some revenge, and some simply wander aimlessly without any purpose.


BENEFITS:
+1 wis, +1 con
Burning soul: Despite being fallen, you still retain the flaming essence you were created with. You have resistance to fire and 1/day you can deal an extra 1d4 fire damage with a melee attack.

DRAWBACKS:
Fallen: You have fallen from Itris' grace, and thus she has clipped your wings. While you can glide small distances at a time, you can't fly and are shunned by her followers.

Fae Kin

As they have with every other race, the humans have mingled with fae, since they seem to have no sense of self preservation. These occasional unions have spawned very few offspring, but there have still been some successful cases. The human blood in these offspring have diluted the mutations just enough to make them look somewhat more normal, but the traces of the fae bloodline isn't erased entirely. These beings have a variety of traits inherited from their fae blood including but not limited to slightly finer features, skin tinted in unnatural looking shades, the small beginnings of horns, larger frames, and more.


BENEFITS:
+2 Dex, and +2 to any stat of your choice or +1 to two stats
Keen senses: Having come from a line with unnatural senses, you inherited some improvements to your vision, hearing, and other senses. Gain +2 to perception.
Eternal bloodline: Fae Kin share how they age with their fae relatives, that being that they don't age a bit after reaching full maturity.

DRAWBACKS:
-2 Con. While humans are technically compatible with some fae, it doesn't necessarily mean they should procreate. Children that come from such a union tend to have minor complications with their health. Usually it's nothing serious enough to be life threatening, but it does make life inconvenient at the best of times.

Warforged

A warforged is created when a soul is artificially placed inside a construct with the proper enchantments and runes to replicate a living entity without actually being a living entity. The warforged, as they are now called, are beings made of metal, wood, and occasionally other materials. Early into the development of the mage kingdoms on Airith the warforged were made for farm work or household chores and similar tasks. Some were designed for more complicated jobs, but for the most part they were created with the intention of making it easier for the mages of Airith to focus on their research. Once the war broke out they were repurposed, and after the massive magical explosion that tore apart the continent some made it out intact enough to continue functioning, and of those who made it out a few gained sentience through varying means.


BENEFITS
+2 con, and +1 to one other ability of your choosing
Mechanical being: Due to your inhuman nature you have no need to eat, drink, or breath. On top of that you are immune to disease and when taking a long rest you only need to spend 6 hours in an inactive, motionless state. When inactive you can still see and hear as normal.

DRAWBACKS
Unfeeling: Being a non-living creature has its upsides, but it also has some downsides. One of these downsides being a lack of emotion. Where normal creatures can at least feel basic fear, happiness, and pain, you feel nothing. This makes beings of the fleshy variety very difficult to interact with at times.
