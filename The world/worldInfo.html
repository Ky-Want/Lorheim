Airobor Caedis

Arobor Caedis: This is a disease that's created when magic from Regnum Infernum seeps into Loreheim and comes into contact with wood. This disease is most commonly known for affecting trees, but it can in some cases affect woody vines. When the magic comes across one such plant and sticks it will start to cause mutations in the wood that flow down to the roots, making their growth increase drastically. Trees with Arbor Caedis can easily overtake entire towns and forests in a matter of weeks. They also change to house a black acidic sap as a defense mechanism that burns through anything it comes into contact with when the bark is damaged. Once the tree expands enough it's roots will form medium sized masses that will slowly take on the shape of whatever living creature the roots encountered the most. One such root creature will be produced per day and will attack anything living around the tree and its roots. Fire and any other normal means of removing trees has proved to be rather ineffective when combating those with Arobor Caedis due to its plentiful supply of sap, instead it's recommended to use spells fueled by holy magic or electricity to destroy the trunk. Once the core is damaged enough the rest of the tree will wither away and die, along with it's evil spawn that rely on it's magic to stay active.

Demon Hunters

The demon hunters make up a specialized series of guilds housing individuals who focus solely on the extermination of demons. Within this guild there are three different primary ranks.

*White rank: After several years of training of extermination missions a black rank may get the chance to promote to a White, though the process is very lengthy and difficult. A black rank is required to go through a series of tests, and even if they succeed the guild master might not deem them worthy of the rank. White rank demon hunters handle the most difficult hunts that involve demons who could very well wipe a village off the face of Loreheim simply by sneezing. Currently there are only five White rank hunters in the world, each one being the equivalent of a level 90 to 100 adventurer.

*Black rank: After about three or four years of going through intensive training the blue ranks are promoted to black. Black rank guild members are generally in charge of training the blue rank newbs and clearing out the less dangerous demons that crop up throughout Loreheim.

*Blue rank: Those in the blue rank are essentially the newbies. They have the least experience and are the most likely to die.

Black Rose Necromancers

The Black Rose necromancers are a group of people who built their way of life around necromancy. They gained their name from their city, Black Rose City. They live without poverty and have managed to exist peacefully in hiding for several generations so far.

Rather than fearing necromancy and viewing it as something taboo, they embrace it. Upon an individual's death their body is donated to the council after the funeral has been held and they are brought back from death to join the work force that keeps their city running. Either that or they join the military force that tirelessly patrols the Black Rose territory, keeping its citizens safe from harm.

Partially due to the well looked upon tradition of becoming a cog in the city's powerful machine after death, reproduction is highly encouraged, and families who are welcoming new children into Black Rose are given plenty of time off of work and any other duties they may have to focus on raising their new addition(s) to the family.

The council is made up of thirteen diverse people who each take charge of a different aspect of the city. For example, there is a head of the undead military force, the undead workforce, the city's finances, the city's production of goods, local order, etc. Rank is also irrelevant when it comes to being a member of the council, instead the focus is on whether or not the individual would be well suited to the job, both morally and skill-wise. If a person is deemed unfit to be on the council both the people and the council can vote to have the person removed and replaced by the next most suitable option.

Whisperers

The Whisperers are essentially dealers in information. While most are loyal to their town or city, there are some who deal information to outsiders as well. For the right price, of course.

The Opal Lotus

The Opal Lotus is a company in Railis that deals in illegal drugs and stolen goods provided by the pirates and alchemists that work under them.
There are currently five ships that collect goods for the Opal Lotus, all of them being pirate vessels that actively raid and sink other merchant ships in order to supply the company with the majority of their goods. There are three other ships that simply transport the stolen goods to port for the merchants on land to take and sell in the surrounding cities and towns.

There are also several hidden away green houses throughout the Pharin and Drasaan kingdoms that grow and supply various mushrooms and other plants which are used to create potent and highly addictive drugs for the Opal Lotus.
