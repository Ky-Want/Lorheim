Healer

Skilled in the arcane art of mending the broken, healers are highly sought after and often sent to the front lines. Unlike medics healers aren't suited for combat at all. They specialize in keeping the people around them alive no matter how many hits they may take, though some have learned a few tricks to help protect themselves when in combat.

Class Features
As a Healer you gain the following features:

Hit Points Hit Dice: 1d6/level
Hit Points: 6 + Constitution modifier
Hit Points at Higher Levels: 1d6 + Con

Proficiencies
Weapons: Simple weapons
Saving throws: Wisdom, Intelligence
Skills: Choose three from Arcana, Medicine, Nature, Perception, Investigation, Acrobatics, Persuasion




level 1 mana points: At level 1 you have 8 points you can use to boost your spells.

Class Ability

*Preferred patient: Some types of creatures are easier to heal for you than others. You gain advantage on medicine checks with preferred patients. Choose one from Fae, Lizardfolk, Human, Demon, Undead, Artificial (eg. Dwarves), and Divine beings (eg. Fallen Astre)

level 1 actives: Pick 3

*Defy death, lvl 1, reaction: 4 mana points, as a reaction you can bring an ally up to 30ft away back to 1 hitpoint after being struck down. This can't be used if they actually die though, only if they are in the negatives and dying. Every additional point used gives an extra 1d6 of healing.

*Restore, lvl 1, action: At will you can heal one ally up to 10ft away for 1d4 + Wisdom. One mana point gives an additional d4, 2 points gives and extra 5ft of range.

*Defensive healing, lvl 1, action: 1 mana point, you can store one healing active in an allies body that goes off if they are hit by an enemy attack. Every additional 2 points used grants an extra use of healing that can be stored.

*Mass restoration, lvl 1, action: 1 mana points, you can send a wave of 1d6 + Wisdom healing out to 10ft. Any allies within range benefit from this ability. 1 extra mana point gives an extra 5ft, and 2 gives an extra 1d6.

*An eye for an eye, lvl 1, reaction: 4 mana points, you can reflect half of the damage that's been done to yourself or an ally from one attack to the attacker. For an extra 4 mana points you can completely reflect the damage dealt to the attacker.


level 1 passives: Pick 2

*Divine aura, lvl 1: All single target healing actives give an extra 3 points of health.

*Well of energy, lvl 1: Gain 1 extra mana point for healing abilities.

*The Gift of Excess, lvl 1: If your healing would boost an ally above full health, half of the extra points rounded down turn into temporary healing. This only applies if the ally was wounded, you can't give an ally with full hp temporary hit points.

*Numb, lvl 1: The dead and dying are rarely a pretty sight, but you've grown accustomed to such scenes. Gain +2 to medicine checks when stabilizing or identifying the ailments of others.

*Rushed aid, lvl 1: Gain proficiency in acrobatics to reach fallen allies.

Catalyst

Catalysts are a rare and ancient faction. These individuals are capable of bolstering others spells and abilities regardless of the type. They are incredibly compatible with every known form of magic despite not being able to wield the various magics on existence. Now, normally that would be seen as a relatively harmless skill, but increasing the effectiveness of others spells is not their only ability. A few among this faction are capable of bonding with monsters, similar to how one would bond with their familiar, but on a larger scale.

Class Features
As a Catalyst you gain the following features:

Hit Points
Hit Dice: 1d6/level
Hit Points: 6 + Constitution modifier
Hit Points at Higher Levels: 1d6 + Con

Proficiencies
Armor: Light armor
Weapons: Simple weapons, and the whip
Saving throws: Wisdom, Dexterity
Skills: Choose three from Animal handling, Survival, Nature, Perception, Investigation, Acrobatics, Persuasion



level 1 mana points: At level 1 you have 8 points you can use to boost your spells.

Class Ability

*Soul bond: You can form a familiar-type bond with up to your wisdom modifier of monsters.


level 1 actives: Pick 3

*Adjust course, lvl 1, reaction: 2 mana points, if you're within 5ft of an ally who would miss with one of their spells or spell like abilities, as a reaction you can make minor adjustments to their soul to help them hit their target. Ally gets to reroll their attack

*Range boost, lvl 1, action: 1 mana point, you can give an ally you come into physical contact with +5ft of range to their ranged spells and spell like abilities for one turn

*Strengthened strikes, lvl 1, action: 2 mana points, you can give an ally you come into physical contact with +2 damage to their spells and spell like abilities for one turn

*Enhance bond, lvl 1, action: 4 mana points, you can push or pull extra mana through the bond now, granting a temporary bonus to one of your or your chosen bonds senses

*Take control, lvl 1, action: 4 mana points, you can take full control of one of your bonded creatures for 1d4 turns. This action leaves you vulnerable, but allows you to manipulate the bonded creature and utilize their abilities as you see fit.


level 1 passives: Pick 2

*Makeshift bond, lvl 1: When making a bond with a creature, rather than taking hours to complete it, you can take the equivalent of two combat rounds to make a temporary bond that will wear of in 1d6 + Wisdom modifier minutes.

*Layered mind, lvl 1: Due to your unique method of bonding with monsters you've gained the ability to shield your mind with those you're bonded to. When you have at least one bond active you gain proficiency with saves vs psychic attacks.

*Higher command, lvl 1: You have a greater command of your bonded creatures than normal. They can follow more detailed commands and are less likely to attempt to break free from the bond if displeased with you.

*Longer reach, lvl 1: Your bound creatures can now be up to 50ft rather than 30ft away from you before the bond becomes strained.

*Speedy alterations, lvl 1: You no longer have to maintain physical contact with your allies throughout your turn while casting spells. Now, just a brief touch will suffice.

Juggernaut

Juggernauts, masters of strength and defense, are warriors who aim to block attacks, taunt enemies, and trap foes with their shield. Juggernauts are uncommon, as most warriors prefer methods of power over defense. They strive to embody their strength within their main weapon, a shield. Their shield represents themselves, their honor, and their spirit for a fight.

Class Features
As a Juggernaut you gain the following class features.

Hit Points
Hit Dice: 1d12 per Juggernaut level
Hit Points at 1st Level: 12 + Constitution modifier
Hit Points at Higher Levels: 1d12 (or 7) + Constitution modifier per Juggernaut level after 1st

Proficiencies
Armor: All armor, shields
Weapons: Simple weapons, martial weapons
Saving Throws: Strength, Constitution
Skills: Choose three from Intimidation, Survival, Investigation, Perception, History, or Athletics




Class Ability:
*Starting at 1st level, you can forge a bond with a shield you wield, using it to perform incredible defensive feats. You must spend 1 hour practicing with a particular shield you want to bond with, which can be done during a short rest. You can't bond with more than one shield at the same time, and bonding with another one ends the bond with the previous one.
The bonded shield gains a +1 bonus to its AC. The bonus increases to +2 at 10th level, +3 at 20th level, +4 at 30th level, and +5 at 50th level. If the shield already has a magical bonus to its AC, choose the highest bonus. The bonded shield also allows you to make a Shield Attack;
*Shield Attack: While donning a bonded shield, you can make attacks with bonus action. You make a melee weapon attack against a creature within 5 feet, and cause 1d4 + your Strength modifier bludgeoning damage on a hit. This damage increases to 1d6 at 10th lvl, and 1d8 at 20th lvl.

level 1 actives: Pick 3

*Lifeline, lvl 1, reaction: 1/fight when an adjacent ally would be hit you may shove them back 5ft and take their place. Any damage that would be inflicted to them that turn is instead taken by you.

*Shield rush, lvl 1, action: 2/fight you may rush at opponent and bash them with your shield. The enemy must make a dex save or take 1d6 bludgeoning per 10ft you move (max of 30ft) and is pushed back 10ft.

*Guarding stance, lvl 1, action: 1/fight you may take a defensive stance. Any attacks made against you have disadvantage for the remainder of the round.

*Rallying cry, lvl 1, action: 3/fight you let out the mighty war cry of your people. All allies who can hear you get a +1 to their attacks.

*Shield bash, lvl 1, action: 5/fight you lean back and slam your shield into the face of your unfortunate enemy. Deal 1d6 and stun them for 1 round unless the enemy succeeds a con save.


level 1 passives: Pick 2

*Shield siblings, lvl 1: You and an ally gain +1 to AC when adjacent to each other

*Unyielding shield, lvl 1: Your stubbornness and resilience has begun to pay off, gain +1 to AC

*Guardian of the fallen, lvl 1: If you're within 5ft of an unconscious ally gain +1 to AC as you force any enemies within 5ft of you and your fallen ally to redirect their attention to you.

*Centered, lvl 1: Being as steady as the shield you wield has become a way of life for you, gain +2 to avoid being knocked prone or pushed back.

*A force to be reckoned with, lvl 1: When making shield based attacks deal an additional 2 bludgeoning damage.
