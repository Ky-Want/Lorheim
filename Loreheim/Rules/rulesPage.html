Souls and Magic: An overview of how they work and why

Souls
Souls exist in every living thing, they are what separates the living from the dead. As a creature develops and grows, so to does it's soul. Not all souls are the same though, much like certain evolutionary traits some souls grow and develop to the point of being flexible and capable of processing things like mana, like a mages soul, while some remain in a set state and only exist to prove that a creature is alive. Plants are a good example of this. Every living plant has a soul, and while these souls aren't flexible enough to manipulate magic, they do still take in mana. Though, the way they take in mana is a little different for some. For some plants, namely alchemical ones, they have evolved to absorb some of the mana that enters their soul like they would nutrients, resulting in the various types of plants useable in potions.

Souls are kind of like a bunch of wires. They act as a magnet and conduit for mana. Normally, mana would simply enter the soul and leave without any change, but when a soul is developed and flexible enough it can process the mana into something more useful.

Now for the reason souls act like magnets for arcane mana. This one is rather simple, the reason for this is Naturae mana, or the essence of souls. Naturae and arcane mana are fundamentally different, but also just similar enough to not clash with each other like moody siblings whenever they come into contact with one another. When arcane mana encounters naturae mana, it's drawn in, but once it enters the thing giving off that essence, the naturae mana naturally pushes it out rather than mixing, much like oil and water.

Each soul is different, so there's always going to be a special case, but these are the base rules for souls.




Magic
Magic is broken up into three primary categories. Arcane, Naturae, and Martial.

Arcane mana is an invisible substance that permeates the air and flows through every realm in existence. Souls naturally draw this substance in like a magnet, only for it to flow back out again in a never ending cycle. Not many can harness it, but all have at least some Arcane mana traveling through their soul at all times. Mages are capable of manipulating their souls to take hold of Arcane mana and shape it while it's leaving their body. The way they shape it defines how the mana is processed and what kind of spell they end up using, and the size and power of their soul dictates how powerful the spell can be. Attempting to cast a spell more powerful than your soul can handle can tear it apart. Similarly if you use too much magic in too short a time span you will wear down your soul and eventually either become unable to cast magic, or enter a comatose state. In the worst case scenario overworking your soul could also lead to death.

The next type of magic is known as Naturae, or life magic for the plebians out there. It's a unique type of mana that, rather than simply existing, is produced by all living things. It is also exceedingly dangerous. In using Naturae one could take control of a life, bring the wrath of nature itself down on some poor fucker, or even summon the dead to head your commands in some cases. Due to the dangerous and unpredictable nature of Naturae magic most have become quite wary of those who can wield it.

Finally we have Martial magic. By altering the shape of their soul they can change the course of the mana flowing through their bodies, giving them some additional advantages over the classes that can actually utilize actual magic.
WARNING: attempting to change the fundamental shape of your soul is a VERY bad idea unless you know EXACTLY what you're doing. When a mage uses magic they use their soul to process the mana inside of them and shape it on it's way out to form their target spell, they don't actually do anything that changes or moves the soul unnaturally. The reason it's so dangerous to change the shape of the soul is because when you do you could cause mana blockages in the body, resulting in affects similar to if you poured lead into a person's bloodstream. Mana becomes very volatile when forced to hold still in one spot. On top of essentially poisoning the person, as it condenses due to the blockage and gains a high enough concentration it can also begin processing on it's own, resulting in additional random effect(s) or just mutating the body of the individual, kind of like wild magic. That is, assuming they live long enough to get to that stage and don't seek help from a highly skilled healer to fix their soul. So anything from transforming into a monster to exploding into a massive fireball can happen. Moral of the story? Don't fuck with souls. You won't like what happens.
